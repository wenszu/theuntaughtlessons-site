Perspective taking

See it their way before you say your way.

Your job is to understand how the other person sees the situation before you respond. Not to agree. Not to compromise. To understand first — because how you see it and how they see it are rarely the same thing.

Exercise brief

15 minutes Use the Let's switch hats CustomGPT to step into someone else's perspective, practice reading their constraints and motivations, and reshape how you'd approach the conversation. When you finish, note one thing you'd say differently now that you've seen it from their side.

The scenario

You are now leading the Olympic project. You will work with people who think very differently from you — different roles, different pressures, different definitions of success.

What feels obvious to you may feel risky or unclear to someone else. Before you push your idea, you need to understand their world first.

Before opening the GPT, choose a real person and situation: a colleague you need to convince, a stakeholder who keeps pushing back, a manager who seems hard to read, or a decision someone made that frustrated you.

Choose your person and situation above before opening the GPT.

Your switching framework

  1. 1 Name their role and pressures. What do they need to protect? What are they accountable for? What would failure look like for them?
  2. 2 Identify what they actually want. Not what they say they want — what outcome matters most to them in this situation?
  3. 3 Spot where your view and theirs diverge. Where are you assuming they see it the same way you do? That gap is where the friction lives.
  4. 4 Adjust how you'd approach the conversation. Given what you now know about their world, what would you say differently?
