Situation

The Olympics is still the world's most recognizable sports property, with unmatched athletes, history, and global reach.

Complication

Recognition isn't translating into gravity — shared moments are fragmenting, clip-first consumption rewards spectacle over meaning, and legitimacy is noisier, so the Games feel less like a global ritual and more like optional content.

Question

How do we restore the Olympics as a rare, earned cultural timestamp — without chasing trends or diluting the brand?

Answer

Rebuild Olympic gravity through (1) ritual design that creates shared moments again, (2) athlete-led story arcs that make stakes legible over time, and (3) one coherent spine — a modern thesis and tone system that every channel reinforces while controlling commercial signals.
