Delivery practice

Make your message feel inevitable.

Your job is to take a strong recommendation and make it land when spoken. Not louder. Not more dramatic. Clearer, calmer, better paced, and easier to follow when the room is watching you.

Exercise brief

15 minutes Use the Speak like Obama Gem to upload a recording, review feedback using the rubric, identify one or two improvements, and iterate on your script and delivery. When you finish, note the one delivery habit you will change first.

The speech

You are closing the Olympic project with a short executive speech. The content is already there: rebuild Olympic gravity through shared ritual, athlete-led story arcs, and one coherent strategic spine.

The workbook script asks you to respect the constraints: neutrality, global sensitivity, sponsorship, rights, and precision. The delivery challenge is to make those limits sound confident, not defensive.

Before opening the Gem, record one version of the speech or choose a short excerpt you want to improve.

Record or choose your speech excerpt before opening the Gem.

Your delivery loop

  1. 1 Upload your recording. Give the Gem the audio or transcript so it can evaluate your spoken delivery, not just your writing.
  2. 2 Review feedback using the rubric. Look for the biggest gap in clarity, pacing, emphasis, pauses, confidence, or executive presence.
  3. 3 Choose one or two improvements. Do not try to fix everything at once. Pick the changes that would most improve how the speech lands.
  4. 4 Iterate until it feels ready. Refine the script, rerecord, and compare versions until your message sounds composed and deliberate.
