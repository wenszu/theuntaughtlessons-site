Advisory board with AI

Before you build one, it helps to understand what this tool is doing. Think of it as a structured way to ask AI for multiple perspectives without letting the conversation become noise.

What is a virtual advisory board?

A set of AI-generated advisors, each assigned a specific role. One thinks like a CFO. Another thinks like a customer. Another thinks like someone responsible for making the project actually work. AI is unusually good at this because you can tell it exactly who to be, and it will respond as that person would. You are not getting one generic answer. You are getting six different people reacting to the same work.

Why does having multiple roles matter?

Most people ask AI one broad question and get one broad answer. Giving AI a role changes the quality of what comes back. A CFO notices things a customer never would. A customer notices things an operator never would. When each advisor is locked into a specific lens, the feedback stops being general and starts being useful. You can weigh it, push back on it, and decide what actually applies.

Who is the orchestrator?

The orchestrator is not another advisor. They do not add another perspective to the pile. Their job is to listen to all the advisors, compare what they said, and decide what matters most. They are the person who turns a room full of opinions into one clear direction.

Why does someone need to make the final call?

More advice can create more confusion. The advisors give you breadth. The orchestrator gives you a decision. Without someone responsible for the final call, you end up with a long list of things to fix and no way to prioritize. The orchestrator is the process having a spine.

How do I choose my orchestrator?

You can choose someone who thinks like you when the decision is yours to own. You can also choose the person best suited to the problem, even if they think differently from you. The key question is simple: do you need your best decision here, or the best decision for this specific problem? Those are not always the same answer.

How do I get the most out of this?

Give the board something real to react to. In this exercise, you will give each advisor a problem statement, an issue tree, and an SCQA. They will each respond from their assigned role. Your job is not to accept every comment. Your job is to decide which feedback sharpens the work and which feedback you can set aside.
