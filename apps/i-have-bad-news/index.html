Difficult conversations

Say the hard thing without making it harder.

Your job is to deliver bad news in a way that is clear, calm, and useful. Do not hide the message. Do not over-explain. Give the person enough truth to understand the decision, enough context to trust you, and enough next step to move forward.

Exercise brief

20 minutes Use the Difficult Conversations CustomGPT to rehearse the message, pressure-test your wording, and practice responding when the other person pushes back. When you finish, note one thing you'd say differently next time.

The scenario

You are the person who has to say it. Something has changed, someone will be disappointed, and delaying the message will only make the situation worse.

Before opening the GPT, choose one real or realistic situation: a missed deadline, a rejected request, a budget cut, a performance concern, a client setback, or a decision someone will not like.

Keep the situation professional. The goal is not to win the conversation. The goal is to preserve trust while making the truth unmistakable.

Complete your scenario above before opening the GPT.

Your delivery structure

  1. 1 Lead with the news. Say the core message in the first one or two sentences. No long runway.
  2. 2 Explain the reason, not every detail. Give the logic that matters. Avoid sounding defensive or vague.
  3. 3 Name the impact. Show that you understand what this means for the other person or team.
  4. 4 Offer the next step. Be specific about what happens now, what support is available, or what decision is needed.
